无明城已经存在了 **150 年**。最早的一批拓荒者在一片靠海的荒地上建立了这座城市。（昨天我大致介绍过城市建立之前的历史，但自那之后这里也发生了许多戏剧性的社会事件。）

基因改造人（gmo people）拥有非常先进的科技，以及在政治上的精妙智慧——因为想要欺骗他们其实非常困难。

最初，这座城市是为 **视力障碍者** 或那些 **无法融入以视觉为主导的人类社会的人** 而设计的。城市的整体结构都是为了让看不见的人能够舒适生活、方便行动。

例如，这座城市非常安静，因为人们需要依靠 **聆听自己的信息来源**。

这种安静一部分来自于一种新的交通系统——  
**迷你磁悬浮列车（mini maglev）**。

列车在空中运行，而车站则在地面。这样的结构有两个好处：

- 它在空中覆盖了大量空间
    
- 同时不会占用地面
    

有限的地面建筑会发出一种 **微弱而稳定的节律声**。  
由于大量交通都在空中运行，恶劣天气也很少影响城市运作。

在地面上，大多数人的房子都建在 **一层**，以方便出入。  
有些比较嬉皮的人甚至在家里装了 **滑梯**，用来进出房屋。

城市的安静也来自于居民的 **自我约束**：  
人们不会为了没有意义的事情占用公共声音空间。

---

无明城的人们拥有丰富的娱乐活动。

他们喜欢在开放空间（像广场一样的地方）聚集：

- 举行政治演讲
    
- 举办音乐会
    

他们也会进行各种运动，城市里有专门设计的设施。

不过，在城市中一直存在一个 **非常严肃的争论**：

**是否应该改变下一代在学校学习的语言。**

---

**激进派**认为，他们应该 **重塑语言**。

他们希望把日常语言中所有 **基于视觉的隐喻、俚语和习语**，全部替换成 **非视觉的表达方式**。

他们认为，如果不这样做，他们的社会就会一直处在一种 **不完整的状态**。

但他们并不认为自己是残缺的。

他们珍视自己被强化的 **嗅觉和听觉**。  
他们的社会更 **包容、更平等**，并且形成了一种深刻的文化，在文明和思想上探索新的疆域。

他们的文学不再被符号和象征所束缚，  
他们的诗歌能够 **更直接地触碰人心**。

然而，许多居民依然因为从小使用的传统语言，而在潜意识里把自己的特征当成一种缺陷，这削弱了他们的自我认同。

因此激进派主张：  
他们需要 **属于自己的语言**。

因为他们已经不再认为自己与那些 **视觉主导的人类** 是同一种存在。

对他们来说：

**海是什么颜色并不重要。  
但海浪永不停息的声音，是每个公民的精神故乡。**

---

而 **保守派**则认为没有必要改变语言。

因为世界上仍然有很多视觉主导的人，他们必须继续与这些人交流，以保持社会的活力。

他们并不介意承认自己的局限。

看不见，就是看不见。

尽管现在有许多 **可穿戴 AI 设备**，可以满足大约 **80% 的视觉需求**：

- 避开障碍
    
- 危险警告
    
- 人脸识别
    

但他们永远无法真正理解像 **蔚蓝（azure）** 或 **祖母绿（emerald green）** 这样的颜色。

他们承认，当面对正常视力的人时，他们在某些方面确实处于 **劣势**，这一点无可争辩。

他们认为：

**有缺陷、处于劣势，并不是罪。  
但盲目的傲慢，会阻碍生存。**

---

故事开始时，一个 **毁容的女孩 Ann** 决定搬到无明城。

她的朋友们都试图劝阻她。（至于她是如何毁容的，那是另一个更长的故事。）

她曾经很漂亮，也一直以为自己并不在乎外貌。

但真正的事实是，她早已习惯了 **美貌和青春带来的特权**。

她曾经很受欢迎，也仍然有几位非常好的朋友——那些曾经与她并肩作战、赢得战争的人。

两年前，她失去了漂亮的脸。

同时也失去了她的恋人。

她始终无法适应新的生活。

她表面上装得坚强而积极，但她的童年朋友 **K**（一直暗恋她）看出了她的精神状态正在崩溃。

于是他决定陪她一起前往无明城。

还有另外两位亲密的朋友也同行。

---

因为 Ann **能够看见**，所以她必须通过：

- **音高测试（pitch test）**
    
- **移民考试**
    
- **半年的“观察期”**
    

这个观察期被写进法律，是因为历史上曾经发生过 **盲人与非盲人之间严重的信任危机**。

那段历史相当混乱。

城市之所以设立很高的移民门槛，也是因为这里实行 **UBI（全民基本收入）**。

居民不需要工作就能生活，因此他们并不希望无限制增加人口。

来自外部世界的盲人，尤其是 **先天失明者**，更容易获得公民身份。

这些移民中有人自称是：

- 音乐家
    
- 诗人
    
- 学者
    
- 哲学家
    
- 性工作者
    

或者 **以上全部**。

（是的，这是一个 **哲学家和性工作者数量几乎一样多的城市**。）

---

在正式成为公民之前，Ann 需要住在 **主城旁边的一座小镇**。

这座小镇与主城 **严格分隔**，原因同样是历史上的信任问题。

在那里，Ann 遇见了一个 **世界上最美丽的女孩**。

但她 **天生失明**。

她在小镇工作，负责 **训练新移民**，同时也结识新的朋友。

Ann 还遇到了另一个人——

那个人还残留着一点模糊的视力。  
他们的料理极其简单，却是世界顶级的美味，因为他们拥有 **惊人的嗅觉**。

Ann 的朋友们并没有真的离开。

他们 **偷偷潜入小镇**。

但他们在夜里找不到 Ann。

因为小镇 **完全没有灯**。

（官方说这是为了让新移民更快适应环境。主城里会有极少量灯光，供少数能看见的居民使用。）

---

Ann 对那个美丽的盲女有着复杂的情绪：

- 嫉妒
    
- 同情
    
- 敬佩
    

而那个 **神秘的人** 给她带来了一种久违的平静。

那个人戴着 **面纱**。

他们在十岁时经历过一场疾病，几乎失去了全部视力，只剩下一点模糊的视觉。

他们和 Ann 可以谈论 **他们曾经共同生活过的旧世界**。

他们告诉 Ann：

他们戴面纱是因为知道她能看见，不想让自己的 **伤疤脸** 吓到她。

---

有一天晚上，Ann 与这两位新朋友在海边 **露营**。

在那里，她第一次感受到一个全新的世界：

没有人注意她异常的脸。  
甚至 **没有镜子**。

那天夜里，K 找到了 Ann。

但他发现她在经历了一整年的噩梦之后，终于重新 **快乐而平静**。

于是他决定暂时不打扰她。

K 对另外两位朋友撒了谎，把他们带去了主城进行调查。

---

几天之后，Ann 发现自己 **爱上了那个神秘的人**。

而对方似乎也对她有感情。

这时她的朋友们回来了，带来了 **一个坏消息和一个好消息**。

坏消息是：

城市里依然 **不信任能看见的人**，  
政府在暗中监控他们。

好消息是：

这个世界的医疗技术已经可以 **几乎完美修复受损皮肤**。

但问题是：

大多数医生都是 **盲人**，  
他们并不会愿意做 **美容手术**。

---

于是，Ann 好不容易得到的平静，再次被打破。

她开始面对新的选择：

留下，还是离开？  
接受自己的脸，还是改变它？  
保护那些能看见的人，还是选择视而不见？  
去爱，还是错过？

# Blind City

Blind City has existed for **150 years**.

The earliest pioneers established the city on a barren coastal plain.  
(I briefly mentioned the history before the city's founding yesterday, but since then many dramatic social events have unfolded here.)

The **genetically modified people (GMO people)** possess extremely advanced technology and remarkable political sophistication—partly because **deceiving them is very difficult**.

---

## The Original Purpose of the City

At first, the city was designed for:

- people with **visual impairments**
- those who **could not integrate into a vision-dominated human society**

The entire urban structure was built so that people who cannot see could **live comfortably and move easily**.

For example, the city is **very quiet**, because residents rely heavily on **listening as their primary source of information**.

---

## Transportation

Part of this quietness comes from a new transportation system:

**Mini Maglev Trains**

These trains run **through the air**, while the stations remain **on the ground**.

This design offers two advantages:

- It covers a large amount of space in the air.
- It does not occupy ground space.

The limited buildings on the ground emit a **soft and steady rhythmic sound**.

Because most transportation occurs in the air, even severe weather rarely disrupts the functioning of the city.

---

## Ground-Level Living

On the ground, most homes are built on **a single level** to allow easy access.

Some more free-spirited residents have even installed **slides** inside their homes to enter and exit.

The quietness of the city also comes from the residents' **self-discipline**.

People do not occupy public acoustic space for meaningless noise.

---

## Public Life and Entertainment

People in Blind City have rich forms of entertainment.

They enjoy gathering in open spaces—similar to plazas—where they:

- give political speeches
- hold concerts

They also participate in many sports, and the city contains facilities designed specifically for them.

---

## The Great Debate

However, the city has long been divided by a serious debate:

**Should the language taught to the next generation be changed?**

---

## The Radical Faction

The **radicals** believe the language should be **reconstructed**.

They want to replace all **visual metaphors, slang, and idioms** in everyday speech with **non-visual expressions**.

They argue that without doing this, their society will remain **incomplete**.

Yet they do not consider themselves defective.

They value their heightened:

- **sense of smell**
- **sense of hearing**

Their society is more **inclusive and egalitarian**, and it has developed a deep culture exploring new intellectual frontiers.

Their literature is no longer constrained by symbols and visual metaphors.  
Their poetry can **touch the human heart more directly**.

However, many citizens still subconsciously view their own traits as a deficiency because they grew up using traditional language.  
This weakens their sense of identity.

Therefore the radicals argue:

> They need **a language of their own**.

Because they no longer see themselves as the same kind of beings as the **vision-dominated humans** outside the city.

To them:

> The color of the sea does not matter.  
> But the endless sound of the waves is the spiritual homeland of every citizen.

---

## The Conservative Faction

The **conservatives**, however, believe there is no need to change the language.

The world still contains many vision-oriented people, and communication with them must continue in order to keep society vibrant.

They do not mind acknowledging their limitations.

If one cannot see, one simply cannot see.

Today there are many **wearable AI devices** capable of fulfilling roughly **80% of visual needs**, such as:

- obstacle avoidance
- danger alerts
- facial recognition

But they will never truly understand colors such as **azure** or **emerald green**.

They admit that when facing people with normal vision, they are indeed **at a disadvantage in certain respects**.

This fact cannot be denied.

They believe:

> Being imperfect or disadvantaged is not a sin.  
> But blind arrogance can threaten survival.

---

# The Beginning of the Story

At the beginning of the story, a **disfigured girl named Ann** decides to move to Blind City.

Her friends all try to stop her.  
(How she was disfigured is another, much longer story.)

She used to be very beautiful and always believed she did not care about appearance.

But the truth was that she had long been accustomed to the **privileges of beauty and youth**.

She had once been very popular and still had several close friends—people who had once fought beside her and **won wars together**.

Two years ago, she lost her beautiful face.

And she also lost her lover.

She never truly adapted to her new life.

On the surface she pretends to be strong and optimistic, but her childhood friend **K**, who has always been secretly in love with her, can see that her mind is slowly collapsing.

So he decides to accompany her to Blind City.

Two other close friends travel with them as well.

---

# Immigration

Because **Ann can see**, she must pass:

- a **pitch recognition test**
- an **immigration examination**
- a **six-month observation period**

This observation period was written into law because there had once been a **severe trust crisis between blind and sighted people**.

That period of history was extremely chaotic.

The city also maintains a high immigration threshold because it operates under **UBI (Universal Basic Income)**.

Residents can live without working, so they do not want the population to grow without limit.

Blind people from the outside world—especially those **born blind**—have a much easier path to citizenship.

Among the immigrants are people who claim to be:

- musicians
- poets
- scholars
- philosophers
- sex workers

—or sometimes **all of the above**.

Yes, it is a city where **philosophers and sex workers exist in almost equal numbers**.

---

# The Observation Town

Before becoming an official citizen, Ann must live in **a small town next to the main city**.

The town is **strictly separated** from the main city, again because of historical trust issues.

There, Ann meets **the most beautiful girl in the world**.

But the girl is **blind from birth**.

She works in the town training new immigrants while making new friends.

Ann also meets another person.

That person still retains a faint trace of vision.  
Their cooking is extremely simple, yet it is among the most delicious in the world because they possess an **extraordinary sense of smell**.

Ann's friends have not truly left.

They **secretly sneak into the town**.

But at night they cannot find Ann.

Because the town has **no lights at all**.

(The official explanation is that this helps new immigrants adapt more quickly. The main city has only minimal lighting for the few residents who can still see.)

---

# New Relationships

Ann develops complicated feelings toward the beautiful blind girl:

- jealousy
- sympathy
- admiration

Meanwhile, the **mysterious person** brings her a sense of peace she has not felt in a long time.

This person wears **a veil**.

At the age of ten, they suffered an illness that nearly destroyed their eyesight, leaving only a faint blur of vision.

They and Ann can talk about **the old world they once shared**.

They tell Ann they wear the veil because they know she can see and do not want their **scarred face** to frighten her.

---

# The Night by the Sea

One night, Ann goes **camping by the sea** with these two new friends.

For the first time, she experiences a completely new world:

No one pays attention to her disfigured face.

There are not even **mirrors**.

That night, K finally finds her.

But he realizes that after a year of nightmares, she has finally become **happy and peaceful again**.

So he decides not to disturb her.

He lies to the other two friends and takes them into the main city to investigate.

---

# A New Choice

A few days later, Ann realizes that she has **fallen in love with the mysterious person**.

And the feeling appears to be mutual.

At that moment, her friends return with **one piece of bad news and one piece of good news**.

The bad news:

The city still **does not trust people who can see**,  
and the government is secretly monitoring them.

The good news:

Medical technology in this world can now **almost perfectly repair damaged skin**.

But there is a problem.

Most doctors in Blind City are **blind**,  
and they are unlikely to perform **cosmetic surgery**.

---

## The Decision

The fragile peace Ann has finally found begins to collapse again.

She must face new choices:

- Should she **stay**, or **leave**?
- Should she **accept her face**, or **change it**?
- Should she **protect the people who can see**, or pretend not to notice them?
- Should she **love**, or **let the chance pass forever**?
