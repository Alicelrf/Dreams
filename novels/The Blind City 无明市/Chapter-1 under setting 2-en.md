# Arrival at Blind City

About an hour remained before sunset on this planet when the four of them finally reached the nearest hilltop from which they could see their destination in the distance.  
The small spacecraft landed crookedly on the ground.

On the vast alluvial plain where the river met the sea, patches of color spread across the landscape—vegetation of uneven heights, square farmlands laid out in quiet order. Dark blue grid-like fields of solar panels shimmered under the fading light. The river lay calmly across the middle of the canvas.

On the opposite bank stood a dense cluster of gray buildings enclosed by walls. Further downstream, near the coast, the shoreline resembled an industrial district. A nuclear power plant’s thick, unmistakable chimney rose against the horizon.

At first glance, it did not seem like a particularly remarkable city. On this planet so similar to Earth, it was simply another settlement.

And yet, this was their destination.

**Blind City.**

A city-state rumored to be inhabited entirely by people who were born blind.  
A mysterious nation that lived apart from all other populations, accepting only invited visitors, its coordinates kept secret.

There had once been countless stories about the place.  
But one by one, those stories had faded away in a world that was slowly drifting toward fragmentation.

---

S took a deep breath and turned to the companions behind him.

“It's here,” he said. “Let's go. We'll have to walk the rest of the way. The area ahead is a no-fly zone.”

---

“K, let's go find ourselves a pint of beer,” said the tall, curly-haired man.

The moment he stepped out of the ship, he rummaged through his coat, found a lighter within seconds, and lit a cigarette. He exhaled deeply—the first satisfying drag he had taken in a week.

---

“Good timing,” another man said from the ship’s hatch.

He was still checking the flight records with his back turned to the others. He didn’t look up as he spoke, his shirt billowing slightly in the evening wind.

“The water tank was almost empty.”

---

“Wait… we really have to walk?”

A short-haired girl stepped out of the ship beside him, dragging several bulky bags with her. She stared toward the distant city with a groan.

“Is there seriously no vehicle?”

And then she smelled the cigarette smoke.

---

“L, you’ve got some nerve saying that,” J laughed as he lifted his own backpack.

“Who was it that wrecked my car in Zombie City and couldn't even fix it?”

---

“Hey! Get that cigarette away from me!”

L laughed and swatted J’s hand aside.

“And you’re the one bringing up Zombie City? You’ve got some nerve yourself!”

The remark triggered a round of laughter. Even S laughed.

K turned and noticed S smiling. The sharp lines of K’s face softened.

---

S chuckled and patted J on the shoulder.

“She’s right this time. I’m on L’s side.”

J noticed S standing there and instinctively held the cigarette farther away before starting to walk off.

---

“Hey, wait a second!”

L suddenly had an idea and pretended to hoist one of her heavy bags onto J’s back.

J immediately realized what she was trying to do.

“Oh no you don’t!” he laughed, ducking his head and running off.

“Carry it yourself!”

---

“S, stop him!”

“Don't run! You might as well carry mine too!”

---

K wiped his glasses and stepped away from the ship.

He followed the three of them as they chased and argued ahead.

The others had already slung their luggage onto their backs.

Together they walked forward—

into the last stretch of sunset before reaching **Blind City**.
