
**1. 嗅觉**

盲人的嗅觉和味觉比非盲人要敏锐得多。

在这个设定之下会产生两个发展方向。

第一，盲人可以通过味道分辨出某种蔬菜的两个亚种，而非盲人却无法做到。

其中一个亚种的蔬菜会改变食用者的气味——包括呼吸、汗液以及尿液的气味。

而这种气味也只有盲人能够察觉。

在盲人与非盲人彼此尚未建立信任的那段时期，

由一些盲人控制的食物供应链会**故意**把这两种蔬菜混在一起。

这样一来，所有非盲人都会很容易被盲人通过气味识别出来。

**2. 听觉**
无明市的居民基本都有绝对音准，没有绝对音准的大部分住在过渡区里。

**3. 触觉**
无明市的建材有丰富的材质纹路，熟悉纹路可以轻松知道是哪一面墙。
## Sensory Adaptations in Blind City

### 1. Smell

Blind residents possess a much sharper **sense of smell and taste** than sighted people.

Within this setting, two developments arise from that difference.

First, blind individuals can distinguish between **two subspecies of the same vegetable** purely by taste or scent, while sighted people cannot.

One of these subspecies has a peculiar effect:  
after it is consumed, it subtly alters the eater’s **body scent**—including the smell of their breath, sweat, and even urine.

This change in scent can only be detected by blind people.

During the early period when **trust between blind and sighted populations had not yet been established**, certain food supply chains controlled by blind residents would **deliberately mix these two vegetables together**.

As a result, any sighted visitor who consumed them could be **easily identified by scent**.

---

### 2. Hearing

Most residents of Blind City possess **absolute pitch**.

Those who do not typically remain in the **transitional zone** rather than living in the main city.

---

### 3. Touch

Building materials in Blind City contain **rich tactile textures**.

Once residents become familiar with these textures, they can easily identify **which wall they are touching**, simply by feeling the surface patterns.
