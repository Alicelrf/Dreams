# Blind City — Character Plot Outline

A and J are recurring protagonists in the **Narcissus Virus universe**,  
so this story functions as an **exploration dream during the AJ equilibrium phase** and does **not involve the main storyline**.

The story is largely **ensemble-based**, but the primary narrative tension comes from **S and K**.

---

# Character-Based Story Threads in *Blind City*

### Chef School — A’s Theme
**Smell · Adaptation · Friendship**

A’s storyline focuses on the sensory world of smell, learning to adapt to the city’s culture, and forming friendships through food.

---

### Choir / Musical Ensemble — S’s Theme
**Hearing · Society · Elites · Struggle**

S becomes involved with musical groups in the city, confronting social hierarchies and personal struggles through sound and performance.

---

### Engineer — K’s Theme
**Touch · Engineering · Safety · Outsider → Insider**

K approaches the city through engineering curiosity.  
His arc involves transitioning from an outsider observer to someone who becomes an essential participant.

---

### Underground Kitchen Smuggler — J’s Theme
**Discovering the City’s Dark Side · Food Supply**

J uncovers the hidden side of the city’s food system, including underground networks that supply ingredients to supermarkets and restaurants.

---

### Public Debate in the City
**Group Trust · Identity**

Public debates reveal tensions about trust between different groups and the evolving identity of Blind City.

---

# Chronological Outline of Events

- A, S, and K enroll in the **“on-board learning package”** offered to newcomers.  
- J chooses to explore the city independently.

- The group attends an **improvised choral performance** together.  
  S is deeply moved and begins to consider joining.

- K develops a strong interest in the **architectural and structural design** of the city.

- A quickly becomes tired of the **standardized communal food supply**.  
  She discovers that official restaurants are extremely expensive, and cooking privately is forbidden unless one obtains a **chef certification**.

- J becomes fascinated by **how blind residents quietly detect people who can see**.  
  He tries to investigate the mechanism behind this.  
  He attempts to imitate blind behavior, but is easily identified.

- After finishing an exam, **A and S** attend a **local aromatherapy relaxation event organized by neighborhood mothers**.

- On their way back, they pass an elementary school and overhear part of a **literature class in a school for blind children**.  
  A cannot resist debating the topic and is eventually pulled away by S.

- A begins applying to **chef school** and prepares for her entrance interview.

- J helps A obtain **rare spices** through underground channels.

- S fails to become one of the **violin performers**.  
  She tries to join an elite choir but is placed in a marginal position, so she eventually joins the **citizens’ choir** instead.

- While studying the **history of the choir**, K learns about a legendary performance that once **called whales with music**.

- The legendary conductor who led that performance is now **over ninety years old** and plans to recreate the event one last time in the spring.

- However, K’s engineering instincts simultaneously detect that **the concert hall structure may be unsafe**.

# Character Development Arc — S

### Stage 1
After being disfigured, S falls into deep pain and a sense of meaninglessness.  
She feels disconnected from the world and unwilling to do anything.

### Stage 2
She begins to hope that **Blind City might offer redemption**, projecting her desires and fantasies onto it.

### Stage 3
She explores, learns, and experiences small successes.  
Her anxiety begins to ease.

### Stage 4
She becomes disappointed after rejection and starts to notice the **coldness and hypocrisy beneath the city’s ideals**.  
Blind City only welcomes sighted people when they serve the city's needs.

### Stage 5
She must decide:

**Should she stay, or leave?**
