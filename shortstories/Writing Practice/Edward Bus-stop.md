At the bus stop on Saturday morning, there are only two figures in school uniforms — one tall, one small.

“You forgot your wallet again? That’s actually insane. I spent half an hour helping you find it yesterday,” the boy says, looking down as she bends over, digging frantically through her backpack.

“Mmmph mmph mmph—”

He bursts out laughing and pulls the scallion pancake out of her mouth.

“I totally forgot I had tutoring this morning. I rushed out,” she says, then lets out a huge yawn.

“What the hell were you doing last night?”

He tears off a piece from the part she hasn’t bitten yet and pops it into his mouth, chewing casually.

“……”

She pretends not to hear him and keeps rummaging.

“Stop looking. Just use my card. Honestly, what am I gonna do with you?”

She grins, already reaching for it, not surprised at all. “Much appreciated, sir.”

Right as her fingers are about to touch it, the card disappears.

“But you have to tell me what you were up to last night, shorty.” Edward lifts the card high over his head.

“Are you kidding me? That’s so unfair!”

Alice springs up, climbing onto the bench at the bus stop, swiping at him. He dodges her flailing arms easily.

Just then, the bus pulls up to the curb.

Edward glances back for a second — and the card is suddenly gone from his hand.

Pssshhh. The bus doors wheeze open.

Alice beams, clutching the bus card, and runs onto the bus.

Edward sighs and pulls out his wallet to dig for change — then notices her backpack still sitting on the bench, wide open.

He looks down at it, shakes his head, picks it up, and follows her onto the bus.
