The Last Dream of 2025

In my dream, my phone issued an emergency alert saying that a tornado had suddenly formed out at sea. At that moment, my friend and I were on an island looking for a cat that one of her customers had lost. She told me she had only recently moved to the island and hadn’t finished setting up her business insurance yet. If the cat was lost, she would have to pay a lot of compensation, and her cat café and boarding business might not survive.

So we searched all over the island, which was full of hills. We climbed stairways and passed through many people’s backyards while looking for the cat.

Eventually we reached a place close to the shore. A lot of people were running back from the seaside and telling everyone not to go any farther—the waves were getting bigger and bigger, and we needed to get inside to take shelter. Soon after that, strong winds arrived, bringing sand and dust like a hailstorm. The sky became dark and chaotic.

We rushed into a guard post near the sea bridge. The post was a little elevated, so it had a great view. From there we could see the tornado moving across the sea 🌪️.

As time went on, more and more water, dust, and debris were swirling through the air, until it was almost impossible to see anything. Only the direction of the sea bridge remained somewhat visible—at least faintly.

Then I saw something strange: giant scallops, maybe more than ten meters tall, lined up in a row, lightly hopping one after another along the sea bridge, heading straight toward the huge tornado! Inside the tornado we could faintly see many golden figures moving.

When the wind briefly weakened and there was less sand in the air, we could vaguely make out something like kitchens in a fairyland, where everyone was happily catching seafood. My friend and I marveled and said that Li Bai’s line “The immortals stand in rows as dense as hemp” must really be true.

The tornado passed by in an instant, and the wind stopped. The giant scallops were gone.

We started worrying again about the lost kitten. Just then, it poked its head out from a gap in the air conditioner exhaust vent of the guard post and meowed.

When we put the cat into its carrier and walked out of the guard post, the sky had already become completely clear—as if nothing had ever happened.
