# Snowpiercer (Anxious Rule Setting)
**20240302**

Last night I had a slightly anxious dream.  
I was on a ski trip with several friends from Waterloo. We were heading to enormous mountains. First we flew to the city, then boarded a train with all our luggage.

The train kept climbing hill after hill. At that altitude it was extremely cold, and the train had to keep moving constantly—otherwise it might not be able to start again.

Because of this, there were **no train stations for transfers** in that region. Instead, trains would run side by side very closely with their doors open, aligning for only a minute or two so that passengers could transfer.

Everyone had to rush during that brief window to move their luggage from one train to another.

After I moved my last suitcase, I went back to check whether I had forgotten anything. I hadn’t—but when I returned to the door, it had already closed.  
I had missed the transfer.

And I had nothing with me.

I asked the crew what I should do. They said I could take the next transfer, which would still bring me to the same destination.

*Snowpiercer?*  
Ah, I think I’ve seen the first movie.

Eventually I did manage to board another train. However, I got into the wrong half of it. The front and back halves of the train would later separate and head toward different destinations.

So I asked the crew for help again. This time they let me off at an actual train station.

There I desperately searched through my many pockets for ID. In the end, all I had was a credit card and my ski ticket—nothing else.

Those were actually the only two things I had chosen to carry with me. Based on my past record of losing things, I didn’t want to risk bringing more important cards.

The crew wasn’t too strict with me. They eventually put me back on the correct train.

In the end I reunited with my friends, and I even got to ski in the dream.  
The hills were incredibly high, almost reaching into the sky.

I could see myself skiing like a cartoon character.  
It actually felt more like a game I once played—**Alto’s Adventure**, a beautiful and soothing game.

## Jail in Germany (Escape Rule)
**20240106**

In my dream I was in a prison in Germany. The rule there was that if you **broke out of prison and were caught again, you wouldn’t receive extra punishment**.

Because of this, I became very passionate about escaping.

I found a partner, and the two of us worked hard on our escape plans.

The explanation behind the law was simple:

> If someone escapes and is captured again, their sentence will not be extended.  
> The reasoning is that **the desire to break free is part of human nature.**

## Climbing Escape Room (Amusement Park)
**20200503**

Last night I dreamed that I **spent the whole night playing**.  
There was ice skating, cooking big meals, and visiting an ice-and-snow amusement park. By the end I was already quite tired.

But the most interesting attraction was a **climbing-wall escape room**.

It was designed like the vertical cross-section of a huge castle, filled with different rooms—some containing treasures, others forming maze-like puzzles.

About ten players were suspended on ropes, climbing upward along the wall. The goal was to reach a **specific exit at the top** to complete the game.

Some rooms could be entered for **resting**.  
Some had **refrigerators and bathrooms**.  
Some rooms even contained **a large tiger**.  
There were **wizards** as well.

It felt like someone had taken an escape room and placed it on a **vertical plane**.

Before climbing, we could even stand below the wall and **make a rough plan** for how to progress.

The castle was enormous.

Meanwhile, in the non-ice areas of the ice park, I saw people **riding bicycles while wearing ice skates**.

Theoretically it made sense—it would **save time switching shoes**.

## Are You an NPC? (Social Rules)
**20200425**

In the dream the world had become like a game, populated by both **human players** and **NPCs**.

Human players could die and kill others, but they didn’t know the overall storyline or their own fate.

NPCs, however, were conscious. They couldn’t die and couldn’t kill. They had to follow the destiny written into the system.

They didn’t fully understand their own destinies—only that some mysterious force would suddenly compel them to do certain things.

Some NPCs lived alongside humans. Yet there was always a faint sense of segregation. When seeing someone, you might wonder: *Is that a human, or an NPC?*

Some NPCs tried to pretend they were human. Some humans tried to pretend they were NPCs.  
The boundary was blurry.

When NPCs had no tasks, they constantly sought excitement to relieve boredom. But once a task appeared, they still had to complete it—reluctantly.

Their settlements were filled with strange architecture designed for thrills. For example, one elevator was just a metal frame with hanging seats. People sat trembling while pressing the floor buttons.

When reaching a floor, you had to swing yourself out of the seat into the corridor.

I asked an NPC to push my hanging seat closer to the exit so that my short legs could reach the doorway.

## War (Faces of War)
**20180304**

In the dream war began without warning.

My city received a **five-minute nuclear strike alert**. The department I worked in was responsible for launching nuclear missiles.

My supervisor—who had once been my mentor studying satellite orbits—lost hope, drank heavily, and shot himself.

That left only me and another operator trying desperately to contact headquarters via telegraph, because we didn’t have the authorization codes.

According to protocol, we had to launch immediately upon receiving the alert.

We raced against time, hoping to fire our retaliatory missile before our own city was destroyed.

After endless identity verification, the final code finally arrived—but by then the expected attack time had already passed.

No missile struck us.

I began entering the launch codes one by one while my partner watched the radar, ready to fire if anything appeared.

We were so tense that we could barely speak.

Although we had trained to launch without hesitation, when the moment truly arrived it was not easy to release a weapon that could decide the fate of tens of thousands of people.

We asked headquarters what to do.

Their final response was:

> “Make the decision based on your relationship with this world.”

My partner and I looked at each other for a long time.

In the end we put on radiation suits and retreated deeper into the bunker, leaving the launcher with only the final code left to enter.

Later the dream showed many scenes of wartime life.  
Although nuclear war never happened, Russia occupied Pakistan. Social media initially demanded war, but soon communications were shut down in the name of security.

People were terrified but tried to continue daily life. Prices soared as everyone hoarded supplies.

Gradually many people quietly moved out of cities. Rural teaching and civil service jobs suddenly became highly sought after.

On the streets everyone walked quickly, yet at night the sound of mahjong tiles could still be heard.

I even joined a mahjong game with my father. In those uneasy times the atmosphere felt strangely exuberant.

The host took out a bottle of fine liquor he had been saving for years and said:

> “If we don’t drink it on days like these, we’d be betraying the time this wine has shared with us.”

## Searching for Someone in a Dream (Dream and Reality)
**20170630**

In the dream I was searching for a childhood friend I had once been paired with in a rural program.

We had hidden together inside a windmill, lying side by side watching light and shadows spin across the blades.

Her grandmother made snacks for us, calling us down to eat. Her grandfather, an old Red Army soldier, would sit peacefully in the sun listening to the radio.

In the dream I tried to track them down through the internet. I eventually saw a photo of my friend and learned that her grandfather had passed away and her grandmother’s health was failing.

When I woke up, I realized something strange:

I had **never actually had such a friend**.

Yet the memories in the dream felt so familiar and comforting.  
Could it be that the past we cling to in reality is also just another dream?

Dreams reveal the illusion of reality—and the reality within illusion.

## Spherical Spacetime (Sci-Fi Idea)
**20170608**

I dreamed about a strange structure of spacetime: a huge spherical space where every point was the same distance from every other point.

In such a space, you could appear anywhere instantly without time passing.

These spheres floated invisibly through the universe like bubbles, allowing humanity to travel between stars far more quickly.

When I woke up, I discussed the idea with a friend studying theoretical physics.

He said that in such a spacetime **relativity would not hold**, so structures like that probably cannot exist in our universe.

## The Bamboo Grove Disturbance (Reality Intruding into Dreams)
**20170607**

I dreamed that I was sleeping on a bamboo bed in a wooden house in a bamboo grove.

During the night, a long bamboo pole suddenly grew through the wall and poked my face, making it itch and hurt. Furious, I got up to find an axe and cut it down.

When I woke up, I immediately realized what had happened:

My cat had jumped onto the bed while I was sleeping.

The “bamboo pole” was almost certainly its tail brushing against my face.

## The Bribeable Monster (Absurd Anxiety)
**20160301**

In a large campus, a two-meter-tall furry monster—something like Chewbacca—was attacking people.

When it caught someone, it forced their arms together and shocked them with electricity for about ten seconds, leaving them paralyzed.

Everyone ran around the campus trying to hide.

Eventually another girl and I locked ourselves inside a small operating room in the hospital, hoping the heavy door would hold.

The monster still found us and began smashing the door in rage.

We didn’t dare leave even after the noise stopped, because it might still be waiting around the corner.

Someone had told us that one person once managed to **bribe the monster with money** right before being shocked and was spared—but it only worked once.

We decided staying inside was safer.

Eventually we escaped and planned to drive far away.

Then I suddenly remembered: I only had a **G1 learner’s license**, not a G2.

Still, dealing with traffic police seemed far easier than dealing with an electric Chewbacca.

## The Black Widow (Absurd Explanation)
**20150528**

In the dream I killed a large bug and was soon bitten by three different spiders.

I looked up treatment in a book and saw an illustration of a large red spider with this explanation:

> “If bitten by this species, there is nothing to be done.  
> Use your remaining minutes to repent and pray.  
> Prevention: perform more good deeds in daily life.”

Well then.

Since I was apparently doomed, I quietly began repenting for my chaotic little life.

## Impressionist Sky & the Gay Theme Park (Aesthetic Wonder)
**20150324**

In the dream a friend and I were walking through a seaside city toward a park.

Looking up, I saw a sky that resembled the ocean: huge blocks of color floating like waves. The colors shifted in bold patches rather than gradients.

Giant white birds circled overhead.

The scene was so beautiful that we stood there watching for a long time.

Later we entered a vast green meadow dotted with golden statues of historical figures—some looked like Newton, Goethe, and Schiller.

Only when we reached the exit did we realize we had just walked through a **gay-themed park**.

## The Curtain Festival (Fairytale Beauty)
**20141209**

In the dream I was wandering through a European town during a **Curtain Festival**.

Along both sides of the stone street hung enormous curtains blowing in the wind—cream, pale pink, floral patterns, stripes, blue-green velvet, gauze, canvas.

The curtains bulged outward so much that the buildings almost disappeared behind them.

Sunlight filtered through layers of fabric, scattering soft colors everywhere.

In the dream I could paint. I carried a sketchbook filled with drawings.

Some foreign girls loved my work and brought me to meet their teacher.

But the old white-bearded man didn’t appreciate it. While they argued with him in a language I couldn’t understand, I quietly smiled, lifted the curtain at the doorway, and continued my journey.
