Last night in my dream, I caused quite a bit of trouble.

I was exploring a deep cave on an adventure. The cave was extremely long and deep. When it became completely dark, I lit an oil lamp to protect myself.

With the light from the lamp, I discovered a gorgeous mural on the cave wall.

I took some photos of it and then returned.

When I told my friends about the discovery, they were all very excited and wanted to see it themselves.

A couple of days later, the police questioned me and asked me to hand over the photos.

It turned out that two of my friends had gone into the cave one night. While they were there, they accidentally dropped an oil lamp or a candle. Normally, you wouldn’t expect that to cause any problems — it was a cave after all, and caves are usually damp and humid. At most, they might have risked choking from the smoke.

But this cave was different.

The entire cave was covered with a certain kind of biological wax or oil secreted by insects.

When the flame from the lamp or candle touched it, the cave suddenly caught fire.

My friends were almost killed in the accident, and the mural was half destroyed.

Because of this, a team of mural restoration specialists needed my photos and my memory of the painting to help restore it.
I felt even more guilty — both for the damage done to the artwork and for the danger I had unknowingly put my friends in.
So the second time I returned to the cave, it was to guide the artists and describe what the mural originally looked like so they could repair it.
When I returned to the cave, I was filled with anxiety and guilt.

The mural was now covered with a layer of soot, and some parts of it had completely disappeared.

At that moment, I saw a familiar figure standing there with his back to me.

He turned around.

It was J.

“Wow, look what you’ve done,” he said with a grin.  
“Do you have any idea how long it’s going to take me to fix this?”

He was clearly teasing me.
