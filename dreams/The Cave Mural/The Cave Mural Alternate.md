I first walked for what seemed like an eternity in the dripping cavernous opening. It was near the edge of a clearing in the forest by my home. A yawning mouth slowly belching its nauseating aroma.

I could hardly resist my morbid curiosity. For weeks I resisted my strange and unsettling urge to spelunk it's depths. Today my guard failed me.

The cavern narrowed uncomfortably as I could hear gibbering insects and the scuttling of thousands of chitinous claws

I felt as though I had to squeeze and crawl my way through, crouching to discomfort, lowering my head and shoulders. My nose now too close to the stench of the oily floor and the lone flame of my lamp flickering, unsettled by it's tilted angle in the awkward position of my hand, as it cast dancing shadows on the caves walls

Just as I had the mind to turn my tail and flee this place, I saw it. First with the scarlet reds, and the subtler colours intermingled in a horrid but stunning display of Artistry

A mural, or fresco, stretched well beyond my view, the likes of which I had never seen. Depicting reverence, but to what I could not understand, to a terrible gaunt figure with swollen head and tentacles. Wings stretched high, blood red eyes and claws outstretched in some terrible ritual

I stood mouth agape, gasping for breath as it dawned upon me how old this must be. Only as I saw those beings participating in the ritual did I truly understand that I must flee. My shaking clumsy hands lost grip on my lamp and the terrible oils burst into a ripping flame

The screaming insects further fueled my now steely determination to flee this place

Slick with waxy oil I slipped, scraping hands and clothes as I desperately clawed my way free of this wretched place, now scoured by the flames

I escaped with my life but in my dreams I am still trapped in that place, and the eyes. The eyes see me, the SEE me. I am not alone in my dreams, NOT ALONE
