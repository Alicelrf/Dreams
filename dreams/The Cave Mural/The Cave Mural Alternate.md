I first walked for what seemed like an eternity in the dripping cavernous opening. It was near the edge of a clearing in the forest by my home. A yawning mouth slowly belching its nauseating aroma.

I could hardly resist my morbid curiosity. For weeks I resisted a strange and unsettling urge to spelunk it's depths. Today my guard finally failed me.

I walked, claustrophobic, and the cavern narrowed uncomfortably as I could hear gibbering insects and the scuttling of thousands of chitinous claws.

I felt as though I had to squeeze and crawl my way through, crouching to discomfort, lowering my head and shoulders. My nose now too close to the stench of the oily floor and the lone flame of my lamp flickering, disquieted by it's tilted angle in the awkward position of my hand, as it cast dancing shadows on the caves walls.

Just as I had the mind to turn my tail and flee this place, I saw it. First with the scarlet reds, then with subtler colours intermingled in a horrid but stunning display of Artistry.

The twisted mural stretched well beyond my view, the likes of which I had never seen. Depicting reverence, but to what I could not understand, to a terrible gaunt figure with swollen head and tentacles. Wings stretched high, blood red eyes and claws outstretched in some terrible ritual. Swollen figures with grave purpose massed around alters for their dreadful tasks.

I stood mouth agape, gasping for breath and mind reeling as it dawned upon me how old this must be. Only as I saw those nauseating figures did I truly understand that I must flee. My quivering and clumsy hands lost grip on my lamp and the putrid oils burst into a ripping flame.

The screaming insects further fueled my now steely determination to flee this place.

Slick with waxy oil I slipped, scraping hands and clothes as I desperately clawed my way free of this wretched place, now scoured by the flames

I escaped with my life but in my dreams I am still trapped in that place, and the eyes. The eyes see me, the SEE me. I am not alone in my dream, NOT ALONE
